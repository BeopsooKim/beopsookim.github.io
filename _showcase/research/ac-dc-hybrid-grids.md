<h3 class="h5">AC-DC Hybrid Power Grid Analysis</h3>
<p>
I develop a sequential AC-DC analysis procedure that transfers a converged operating point
from power-flow analysis into fault and harmonic studies.
</p>
<p class="small text-muted mb-2">
Core questions: how converter controls, current limits, and blocking conditions determine fault
response, and how converter coupling paths and finite DC-side admittance determine how AC
harmonics appear as DC-side voltage ripple.
</p>
<ul class="small mb-2">
    <li>Sequential AC-DC power flow with explicit converter control modes and operating limits.</li>
    <li>Power-flow-initialized AC and DC fault analysis with converter current limiting and blocking.</li>
    <li>Frequency-domain analysis of AC harmonics and DC-side voltage ripple through converter coupling paths.</li>
    <li>Evaluation on public test systems, with selected AC fault and harmonic results compared against external reference models where available.</li>
</ul>
<p class="small mb-0">
Representative work:
<a href="https://doi.org/10.1038/s41598-024-74166-1" target="_blank">DC circuit-breaker allocation</a>,
HVDC trend analysis, and sequential hybrid AC-DC grid analysis.
</p>
